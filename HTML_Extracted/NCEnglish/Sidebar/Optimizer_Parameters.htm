Optimizer Parameters

There are two different types of Optimizer Parameter Screens depending on whether you are using a Machine that needs Saw Optimization or a Machine that needs Nesting.

Saw Optimization

There are four tabs in the Saw Optimization Optimize Properties screen:

Patterns Tab

The Save option here will allow you to Save any changes made in this area directly to your selected Primary Machine in the Machine Catalog.

Following are the main options in the Patterns Tab:

  1. You can control the which face is up on the sheet. There are three options:

    1. Finished Side Down

    2. Finished Side Up

    3. Most Operations Up

  2. You can control whether you want your first cut on the saw to be a Rip (Cut with the grain), a Cross/Y Cut (Cut across the grain), or Either.

  3. You can control how you want your Strips sorted. The image below has 2 Strip sets, the vertical containing a single strip and the horizontal containing 5 strips.

A Strip is single element from a Strip Set. A Strip will always have Cross-Cuts or (Y-Cuts) and possibly Z-Cuts.

All the cuts that produce a part on this pattern are Y-Cuts except the Z-Cut displayed in red. A Y-Cut or Cross-Cut runs in the opposite direction of a strip producing either a Part or setting up to produce a Part.

  1. You can control how you want your Parts within the each Strip to be sorted.

  1. You can eliminate Z-Cuts. A Z-Cut is the "Third Phase" cut on any given strip. A "Z-Cut" and "Third Phase Cut" are the same as long as they are cut from the 1st strip set in a pattern. However, we allow the strip series to change directions a maximum of 6 times, the Z-Cut could occur on the 3rd, 4th, 5th,6th, 7th, or 8th phase. Allowing Z-Cuts can cause a dramatic improvement in Yield but at the cost of time spent on the Saw. This can cause the operator to make multiple cuts to bring a part to its finished size.

Eliminating Z-Cuts will affect the type of patterns that are generated but it will not reduce the phase count. Make certain to set up the Phase Count for your Saw in the Machine Catalog Setup Tab.

  1. You can eliminate T-Cuts.

T-Cuts are cuts made to break a sheet apart prior to Ripping and/or Cross-Cutting. Allowing T-Cuts can cause an improvement in Yield but like Third Phase Cuts can also cost time spent on the Saw.

  1. You can control whether Offcuts and Waste are cut from strips first or last.

  2. You can control the Quality of your Optimization. Moving the slider to the right improves the results of the Optimization but slows the optimization process.

  3. Yield and Labor are used to determine how well the job will be booked (stacks). A selection of Labor would favor fewer cycles over Material optimization. A selection of Yield would favor less Material over fewer cycles.

Make certain to set up Booking for your Saw in the Machine Catalog Setup Tab.

 

Restrictions Tab

There are two main option in the Restrictions Tab:

  1. You can control the Minimum and Maximum Strip Lengths and Widths the Optimization returns. Some Saws have limitations, this allows you to instruct the S2M CENTER Optimizer on your Saws limitations.

  2. You can control the Minimum and Maximum T-Cuts Lengths and Widths the Optimization returns. Some Saws have limitations, this allows you to instruct the S2M CENTER Optimizer on your Saws limitations.

Trim Tab

There are two main options in the Waste/Trim Tab:

  1. You can control the width of Trim (Dust Cut) around each edge of your sheet.

  2. You can control the width of Trim (Dust Cut) around each edge of your offcuts.

Offcuts Tab

Following are the possible options on the Offcuts Tab:

  1. You can control the Minimum Offcut Lengths and Widths the Optimization returns. This option is available with Optimization, Block Nesting, and TrueShape Nesting.

  2. You can control if you Prefer Full Sheets or Offcuts.

  3. You can control if you want the Offcut Bin selection to favor yield or have fewer bins. Basically if you favor yield then the sawyer may have to pull from several different bins where the fewer bins option means the sawyer may only pull offcuts from a few bins.

  4. You can control whether you prefer to use the largest Offcuts over the smallest.

  5. You can control whether to only use an Offcut if it use would not create a new Offcut.

Nesting

There are three tabs in the Nesting Optimize Properties screen:

Patterns Tab

Following are the main options in the Patterns Tab:

  1. You can select your Nest type. The standard Nest Type is Block nesting.

  2. You can Restrict the Rotation in TrueShape to 90 Degrees. This is the rule that allows you to restrict TrueShape Nesting to only be able to Rotate Parts on 90 degree increments. This restriction, if disabled, can significantly increase processing time.

  3. You can control the Quality of your Nest. Moving the slider to the Right improves the results of the Nest at the cost of speed of nesting.

  4. You can control the Pack direction of your Parts in the nest with this option.

Horizontal

Vertical

  1. Common Line Cutting. This option allows you to turn on Common Line Cutting, set your Maximum Retrace, and set whether Small Parts are retraced.

Trim Tab

Following are the options on the Trim Tab:

  1. You can control the width of Trim (Dust Cut) around each edge of your sheet.

  2. YOu can control the width of Trim (Dust Cut) around each edge of your offcuts.

  3. If you are using TrueShape Nesting then you can also set the True Shape Nest Sheet Boundaries here.

Offcuts Tab

Block Nesting

TrueShape Nesting

Following are the options in the Offcuts Tab:

  1. You can control the Minimum Offcut Lengths and Widths the Nesting returns.

  2. You can enable the Create Offcuts function. There are two options:

    1. Outline Offcuts - This option will locate available offcuts within the sheet largest to smallest and outline cut them into the largest possible offcuts honoring your machine and offcut settings.

    2. Square Off Sheet - This will add an additional Route operation to your Nests to Square off your Sheet leaving a single Offcut.

  3. For Block Nesting you can control if you Prefer Full Sheets or Offcuts.

  4. For Block Nesting you can control if you want the Offcut Bin selection to favor yield or have fewer bins. Basically if you favor yield then the sawyer may have to pull from several different bins where the fewer bins option means the sawyer may only pull offcuts from a few bins.

  5. For Block Nesting you can control whether you prefer to use the largest Offcuts over the smallest.

  6. For Block Nesting you can control whether to only use an Offcut if it use would not create a new Offcut.

  7. If you are using TrueShape Nesting you will have some additional options here. Prioritize Sheets instructs the TrueShape Nester in what priority to utilize the available sheet goods. You can Prioritize by Price, Size, or Best Fit. Let’s say you Add Offcuts to the Job and you had a 100 potential Offcuts that could be used for your Optimization run and each of these varied in both Price and in Size. The True Shape Nester wants to know what you want to use first. Do you want to use up the Cheapest Offcut Sheets/Normal Sheets first (Prioritize by Price) or do you want to use up the Smallest Offcut Sheets/Normal Sheets first (Prioritize by Size) or do you not care and just want to get the best Yield from the Offcut Sheets and normal Sheets (Prioritize by Yield).
