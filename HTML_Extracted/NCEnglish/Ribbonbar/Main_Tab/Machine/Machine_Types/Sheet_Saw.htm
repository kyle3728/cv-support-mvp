Sheet Saw

This Machine Type is specifically for Saws. Following are the Setup Tab options for Sheet Saws:

Setup Tab - Sheet Saw

Blade Group

  • Rip Cut Kerf - Use this value to specify the Kerf width of the Rip Blade on your Saw.

 

  • Cross Cut Kerf - Use this value to specify the Kerf width of the Cross Cut Blade on your Saw.

  •  Cut Clearance Travel - Specifies how far the Saw needs to move past the Edge of the Material to complete the cut. Most Saw Links do NOT require this information.

 

Booking Group

  • Booking Max Height - Specifies the Maximum Thickness of Material your Saw will accept.

 

  • Booking Min Sheets - Specifies the Minimum number of Sheets allowed in a Book (Stack) on your Saw.

 

  • Booking Max Sheets - Specifies the Maximum number of Sheets allowed in a Book (Stack) on your Saw.

 

+Label - Label Group

See Sheet Router

+Label - Image Group

See Sheet Router

Optimization - Complexity Group

  • Optimization Quality - Controls the Quality of the Optimization of your Parts.

    • Fastest - This setting offers the fastest Nesting at the computer, sacrificing Yield.

    • Fast - This setting offers a slightly slower speed at the computer improving yield slightly.

    • Good - This setting offers a balance between speed at the computer and Yield.

    • Better - This offers slower speed at the computer with increased improvement to Yield.

    • Best - This offers the slowest speed at the computer with the best Yield results sacrificing speed at the computer.

  • Booking Priority - Sliding to the left favors Yield (less books) while sliding to the rigfht favors Labor (more books).

Make certain to set up Booking for your Saw in the Machine Catalog Setup Tab.

 

  • Max Phases (Turns) - Sets the maximum number of Phases your Saw can handle.

Most older saws have a maximum of 3 phases. Make sure to check with your Saw Manufacturer to find out how many Phases (Turns) your Saw will support.

  • Eliminate Z-Cuts - Allows you to eliminate Z-Cuts. A Z-Cut is the "Third Phase" cut on any given strip. A "Z-Cut" and "Third Phase Cut" are the same as long as they are cut from the 1st strip set in a pattern. However, we allow the strip series to change directions a maximum of 6 times, the Z-Cut could occur on the 3rd, 4th, 5th,6th, 7th, or 8th phase. Allowing Z-Cuts can cause a dramatic improvement in Yield but at the cost of time spent on the Saw. This can cause the operator to make multiple cuts to bring a part to its finished size.

 

Eliminating Z-Cuts will affect the type of patterns that are generated but it will not reduce the phase count. Make certain to set up the Phase Count for your Saw in the Machine Catalog Setup Tab.

  • Eliminate T-Cuts - Allows you to eliminate T-Cuts. T-Cuts are cuts made to break a sheet apart prior to Ripping and/or Cross-Cutting. Allowing T-Cuts can cause an improvement in Yield but like Z-Cuts can also cost time spent on the Saw.

 

  • Part Size Tolerance - Depending on possible unit conversion variances during transfer to the S2M Center from your design/engineering product you may have parts you consider the same size but may have a slight variance in size. This allows you to set a Tolerance for which the S2M Center will consider the Parts the same size.

Optimization - Sorting Group

  • First Cut Priority - Allows you to control whether you want your first cut on the saw to be a Rip (Cut with the grain), a Cross Cut (Cut across the grain), or Either.

 

  • Strip Sort - Allows you to control how you want your Strips sorted within a Strip Set.

 

  • Parts in Strip Sort - Allows you to control how you want your Parts within each Strip to be sorted.

 

  • Offcuts First - Allows you to specify that Offcuts should come off the Strips first rather than last.

  • Height of blade over mat - Allows you to establish the Height of the Saw Blade over the Material on the Altendorf Elmo or Martin T74 Saw Link. Set this to match the setting on your Saw.

  • Max pos of rip fence - Allows you to establish the Maximum Position of the Rip Fence with the Altendorf Elmo or Martin T74 Saw Link. Set this to match the setting on your Saw.

  • Max l/w ratio for X-cut - Allows you to establish the Maximum Length/Width Ratio for cross cuts on the Altendorf Elmo or Martin T74 Saw Link. Set this to match the setting on your Saw. Set this to match the setting on your Saw.

    • Excerpt explaining the Ratio value from Altendorf:

If the ratio length / width of the part exceeds a value of 4 or 5, its width has to be cut at the rip fence, otherwise the crosscut fence could give you no exact 90 degree angle.

Examples:

  • The material is sized 700mm x 500mm, the workpiece has to be 660mm x 480mm:

    • Both cuts are performed at the crosscut fence.

  • The material is sized 1200mm x 200mm, the workpiece has to be 1150mm x 190mm:

    • The length cut of 1150mm is done with the crosscut fence, but the width of 190mm is cut at the rip fence, because the 200mm are too narrow to give an exact 90 degree position for the cut. Therefore the postprocessor parameter “ratio length/width workpiece”.

    • Some identical parts have to be cut out of the rest of a stripe: For instance 7 identical parts have to be cut. For 6 parts the rip fence gives the measure, the 7th part is turned around and cut at the crosscut fence. An exception might be that the 7th workpiece is smaller than 146mm or the length/width ratio forces the part to the rip fence.

  • Allow Negative Waste - This option will appear when a Casadei CN04, CPOUT, Gabbiani Prima, Homag Espana CH03, Macmazza SEZ2000, Macmazza Tork MP70, SCMI Sigma 65, SCMI x915, SCMI z32, or SCMI z45 NC Links are selected. Negative Waste can be created when Grain Matching.

    • Each cut in the figure below is considered a “Gap Cut”; a gap cut is a cut required to maintain the gap between 2 parts. This machine’s kerf is set to 1/4”. Therefore the cut instruction for the 3/4” gap cut would be a 1/4” waste piece. When part 1A is cut it leaves a 1/2” gap before 2A. To remove the 1/2” the machine must cut a 1/4” piece of waste (1/4” waste + the 1/4” kerf = the 1/2” gap).

    • In the figure below the 1/2” and 3/8” gap cuts are considered Negative Waste. Negative waste is output red because it requires special saw instructions. Using the logic described in Gap Cuts, the 1/2” gap requires a 0” cut and the 3/8” gap requires a 1/8” cut. Since the typical saw controller cannot accept either a 0 or negative cut these have to be handled with special cutting instructions. Since only some saws can process negative waste this feature is turned off by default. For saws that cannot process these instructions the negative waste gap cuts are reduced to equal the saw kerf. This would affect the 1/2" and 3/8” gaps above, but not the 3/4" gap.
