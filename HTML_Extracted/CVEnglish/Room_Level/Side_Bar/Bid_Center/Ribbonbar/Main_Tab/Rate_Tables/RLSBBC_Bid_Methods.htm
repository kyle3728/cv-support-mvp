Bid Methods

This is a list of the Bid Methods used by the Bid Center. The value that each item returns is typically multiplied by a value you enter during setup.

New Bid Methods cannot be created by a user in CABINET VISION, nor can the existing ones be changed.  

There are over one hundred Bid Methods plus any Custom Labor Schemes you have created, that you can select from; they are as follows:

% of Material Cost

This Bid Method takes the value you enter (Whole Number representing the Percentage) and multiplies it by the Total Cost of Materials used in the Job. Material Cost is returned and calculated from the highlighted settings below in the Material Manager.

Let’s break these highlighted areas down.

First let’s start with the Default Markup and Default Tax Rate. The Default Tax Rate comes from the Tax Rate either entered here by you for the Material or is brought over by the Vendor selected for the Material. The Default Markup is either entered by you or brought over by the Vendor selected for the Material.

The Tax Rate percentage is only applied to individual Materials if the Sales Tax field is set to True for that Material. Typically, you will only select Tax for those Materials that "you" pay tax on. There is a separate area in the Bid Center to calculate the Tax your "customer" pays.

The Default Cost field in the Materials Properties area represents the selected Units Cost. So, in the example above the Cost is per Sheet. This Cost is either entered by you or is brought over by the Vendor selected for the Material.

The example below represents one Sheet of the Material we have been looking at. In this case the price will come through to the Bid Center for one Sheet as:

  • 1.00 Sheets x $17.00 = $17.00

  • 2% Default Markup = $.34

  • 0% Tax = $0.00 (Because Sales Tax is False)

  • Total = $17.34

The final Material Property that will affect % of Material Cost is the Waste field. The Waste Percentage entered for the Material in the Material Manager will be added, in this case, to the number of Sheets represented in the Job.

% of Running Total

This Bid Method will allow you to specify a % of the Running Total of the Bid Methods directly above it in the list of Bid Methods in the Rate Table.

This Bid Method is similar to % of Sub Total below but does not have a restriction for Catalog assignment.

% of Sub Total

This Bid Method will allow you to specify a % of the Subtotal of the Bid Method directly above it in the list of Bid Methods in the Rate Table.

This Bid Method only works when the Rate Table is assigned to your specific Catalog(s). To do this click Catalogs in the sidebar from inside the Bid Center. Go into Internal Mode and select the Catalog. You will see the Rate Table option here. Simply click in this field and select the Rate Table utilizing % of Sub Total in it.

Use Door Catalog Pricing

This Bid Method does not use the Value field. It takes the Prices you enter for Doors, Drawer Fronts, False Fronts, and Panels and their sub-components and totals them for the Job.

There are several places where this data is retrieved.

Door Catalog

The first area where Door Catalog Pricing is retrieved is from the Door Styles Price fields. You can add a price either "Per Door” or "Per SqUnit” or even a combination of both. The highlighted portion of the image below shows these fields.

Door/Drawer Front Material Schedules

The next area where Door Catalog Pricing is retrieved is from the Material Schedule(s) assigned to the Door Style. You can add a price either Per Door (Cost/Door) or Per Square Foot or Meter (Cost/SqFt/SqM) or even a combination of both. These fields can be used to add Door Pricing Up-charges based on the Material(s) utilized by the Door Style. The highlighted portion of the image below shows these fields.

Door Profiles

The next area where Door Catalog Pricing is retrieved is from the Profile(s) assigned to the Door Style. You can add a price either "Per Door” or "Per SqFt” or even a combination of both. These fields can be used to add Door Pricing Up-charges based on the Profile(s) utilized by the Door Style. The highlighted portion of the image below shows these fields.

The Per SqFt Price entered here represents the Square Footage of the Door that the Profile is assigned to.

Use Drawer Catalog Pricing

This Bid Method does not use the Value field. It takes the Price you enter for Drawer Box(es) in the Drawer/Roll Out Construction and totals them for the Job. The highlighted portion of the image below shows the entry field.

Use Molding Catalog Pricing

Similar to % of Material Cost this Bid Method pulls pricing from Molding Materials (Molding you Buy) in the Material Manager. See the Use Profile Catalog Bid Method for Molding you make from the Molding Manager.

Use Part Catalog Pricing

This Bid Method does not use the Value field. It takes the Price and or Formula you enter for Part(s) in the Part Manager and totals them for the Job. When you are in Internal Mode the Part Pricing will be broken out at the main level of the Bid Center. Parts will be sorted in the list alphabetically by the Part name.

Parts are also broken out for each Cabinet in the Catalog Level of the Bid Center.

Use Profile Catalog Pricing

This Bid Method does not use the Value field. It takes the Price you enter for Molding(s) in the Molding Manager and totals them for the Job. The Cost Per Job field would be a one time Cost when the selected Molding is used in a Job. Cost Per Strip would represent each Piece of Molding. Cost Per FT/Meter would represent the Price you charge for each Foot/Meter of Molding. The highlighted portion of the image below shows the entry fields.

Use Roll Out Catalog Pricing

This Bid Method does not use the Value field. It takes the Price you enter for Roll-Out(s) in the Drawer/Roll Out Construction and totals them for the Job. The highlighted portion of the image below shows the entry field.
