Assembly Sheet -

Assembly sheets are special Reports designed for your assemblers. They will typically have a picture of a single Assembly in the upper right corner of the printout, with all the Parts that go to that Assembly down the left side of the printout, and all the specifications for that Assembly along the bottom of the printout. See the Assembly Sheet Layout topic for more information.

You also have the ability to create your own Assembly Sheets which are stored as Templates in the Drawing Library and can be retrieved by clicking the Change... button in this area.

To Print or send "To Drawing" an Assembly Sheet for one Assembly in a Room, select the Assembly to print, and then click on Print or To Drawing.

To Print or send "To Drawing" Assembly Sheets for only some of the Assemblies in a Room; select the Assemblies to print with your mouse while holding down the "Ctrl" key, and then click on Print or To Drawing.

To Print or send "To Drawing" Assembly Sheets for all Assemblies in the Job, select the "Entire Job" option and then click on Print or To Drawing.

To Print or send "To Drawing" Assembly Sheets for all Assemblies in a Room, select the "Current Room" option and then click on Print or To Drawing.

Controlling Assembly Sheet Face View Layers

You can access the Assembly Sheet Layers to edit the content from the Reports area by right clicking on an open area and selecting Properties. The screen pictured above should then appear. This view is editable in the same way that you can make changes to Layers. The printer icon will be used to determine whether that particular item is visible on the printed page. If the printer icon is lit, that item will appear, if it is dark, that item will not be printed. The color block can be used to determine the color that will be used for that item. The line type can be selected by clicking on the small sample line and then choosing a line type. Finally, some items will be editable. You can click on Shop Annotations, Annotations, or Dimensions and the edit button will become active. By clicking on Edit, you can control details of that item.
