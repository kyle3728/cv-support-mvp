Adding The Parts to The "Part Manager"

  1. From the System Level (opening screen) of CABINET VISION click on Part.

  1. Click on the "+" in front of "Assembly" then click on the "+" in front of "Case" and then click "Exposed".

We will place the parts for the wood top under the Assembly Parts Material Schedules. This way if we are using an "Oak" Assembly Parts Material Schedule, the wood top will be "Oak".  Or if we are using a "Maple" Assembly Parts Material Schedule, the wood top will be "Maple".  This makes more sense than placing the parts under "Counter Top" as this would create all kinds of problems with our Counter Top Material Schedules.

  1. Click the New option in the ribbon.

Look at the screen behind the "New part Name" screen. Notice the column named "Description", and then under this it lists the part names you see when you print your cut-lists.  Notice the column named "Name" and that it lists the short "names" that you use when tracing through the Object Tree, to get your code. The name it is asking for here is the short name you will use when writing code. When adding parts to the Part Manager, you want to keep the names short. With short names you don’t have so much to type when writing code.

  1. Type in wt for Name and Wood Top for the Description and then click on "OK".  It can be caps or small letters it doesn't matter.

  2. Enable all the Exterior options for this Part.

We will now add a new part type called "sk" (for skirt); this will be the skirting that goes around the wood top.

  1. Click on the New option in the ribbon. Type in sk for the Name and Skirt for the Description and then click on "OK".

  2. Enable all the Exterior options for this Part.

  3. Click on the "Close" button in the upper right hand of your screen.

We have added the Parts to the Part Manager under "Assembly – Case – Exposed".  These parts will now show up in our Assembly Parts Material Schedules. Since we have not selected a Material to make these parts out of, CABINET VISION has assigned the "Default" Material to these Parts. We will now go into the Assembly Parts Material Schedule and assign the correct Materials to these Parts.

  1. Click on Material Schedules.

  2. Select "Assembly" in the Material Schedule Type Selector.

  3. Select the Material Schedule you are most likely to start a job with, from the Material Schedule Selector.

  4. Click on the "+" in front of "Case" and then the "+" in front of "Exposed".

You will see two new parts on the list of exposed parts.  The "Wood Top" and the "Skirt".

  1. Click on "Wood Top" and select a panel stock to make it out of and drag it over. Then click on the "Skirt" and select a board stock to make it out of.

  2. Close the "Assembly Parts Materials Schedule" screen.
