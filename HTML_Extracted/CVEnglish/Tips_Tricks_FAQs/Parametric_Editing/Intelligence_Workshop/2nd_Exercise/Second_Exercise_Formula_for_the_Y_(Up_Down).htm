Formula for the Y (Up/Down)

Look at your cabinet from a Face ortho view and click on the second BOARD.  Notice where the reference point is located.  Click on some of the other parts in the cabinet (especially the deck).  Notice where the reference point is located.  When writing code for the position of a part it is important to know where the reference point for that part is in relation to the reference points for the other parts.

Looking at the cabinet from a front ortho view there are several formulas that will work.  We could say the Y is equal to the width of the front toe (the front toe is laying down so its "Width" is running up and down, not left and right) plus the thickness of the deck.  So the formula could be width of front toe plus thickness of deck.  Or, if you click on the deck and then you click on the second BOARD you will notice that they have the same Y position.  So we could say the Y of the board is equal to the Y of the deck.  We will use the second formula the Y of the deck.

  1. Go to the Object Tree and open it up until you see the deck.

  2. Click on the Deck and then click on the Y parameter.  Trace it through the tree and write it down, it should be "Cab.Case.De.y".

  3. Click on the second BOARD.  Then click on the Y parameter.

  4. Click on "Edit".

  5. Change from a "Value" to an "Equation".

  6. On the THEN line type in the code you traced through the tree.

  7. Click on the "Test" button and it should say "Condition returned: True.  Equation returned: 5 or whatever value you were expecting.  Click on OK.

Look at your cabinet in 3D does it look OK?  If not you will have to trouble shoot what you just did.
