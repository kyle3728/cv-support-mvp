Second Exercise for the X Part 3

Our formula is one half the width of the cabinet minus one half the width of the BOARD, or width of cabinet /2 – width of second BOARD /2.

We could also say width of cabinet minus width of second BOARD then the whole thing divided by two.

When you trace it through the tree:

Width of cabinet / 2 – width of second BOARD / 2 it equals Cab.Dx/2 – Cab.BOARD@2.DX/2

Width of cabinet minus width of second BOARD then the whole thing divided by two would equal (Cab.Dx – Cab.BOARD@2.Dx) / 2 same thing really.

We’ll use the first one Cab.Dx/2 – Cab.BOARD@2.DX/2 as our code.

  1. Go to the Object Tree from the Assembly Level (click on one of the ortho views and then click on "Object Tree").

  2. Click on the second board (the bottom one).  Click on the X parameter.  Click on "Edit".

  3. Change it from a "Value" to an "Equation".

  4. On the "THEN" line type the code. Cab.Dx/2 – Cab.BOARD@2.DX/2.

  5. Click on the test button and it should say "Condition returned: True.  Equation returned: 16 or whatever value you were expecting.  Click on OK.

Look at your cabinet in 3D does it look OK?  If not you will have to trouble shoot what you just did.
