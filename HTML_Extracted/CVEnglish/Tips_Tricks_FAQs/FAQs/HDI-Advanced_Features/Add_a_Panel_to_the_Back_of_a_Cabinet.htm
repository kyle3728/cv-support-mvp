Add a Paneled Back to the Back of a Cabinet

Draw a Peninsula Wall, drag and drop Cabinet(s) into place on both sides of the Wall.

We are going to create a panel from an actual Cabinet.

  1. Select the Cabinet that you want to be the panel and take the Cabinet to the Section Editor.

  2. Go to the option Modifications | Default Assembly and click Yes.

  1. While in Section View, go to the option Modifications | Hide Parts and select Case Parts, Interior Parts, Toe Parts.

  1. Go to the Properties tab in the ribbonbar and select the Toes option and change the Toe Height to 0.

  2. Select the Bottom Rail and change its Width to cover the Toe to your shop specifications.

  3. You can now add Doors and Mid Stiles and Rails using the Section Editor.

  4. Use the Return option in the ribbonbar to return to the Plan View of your Room. With the Cabinet still selected, change the Cabinets depth to ¾”.

  1. You have now placed a panel on the back on your Cabinet that can be sized to fit multiple Cabinets.

Utilizing this method with the Wainscot Panel FAQ you will have total control.
