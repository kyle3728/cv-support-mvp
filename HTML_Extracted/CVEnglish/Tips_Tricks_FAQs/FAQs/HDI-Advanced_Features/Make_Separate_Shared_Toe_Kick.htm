Make Separate Shared Toe Kick

I construct my Cabinets with a Separate Shared Toe Base. How do I do it?

As with most things in CABINET VISION, there are various ways to accomplish this. The easiest is to create your toe kick as a separate unit and place it after the Cabinets are designed. After your design is finished, you know where the Cabinet boxes collide and it makes it a "snap" to drop in your toe kicks. Here’s a quick summary of what you will need to do:

  1. Take a Cabinet to the Section Editor and use the Modifications | Hide Parts option to hide all Parts except for the Toe Parts.

  2. Set the height of the Cabinet to match your toe height.

  3. Save the Cabinet back, with Preserve Size checked, into the library with a unique name, for example "Kick Box".

  4. Now, Setup a new Construction Method for your Cabinets and set it up with no toe kick.

  5. Next, drop your default Cabinet that has the auto-fill property enabled into a Room.

  6. Right Click on the Cabinet and Select "Save As".

  7. Go to Advanced Properties and set the Cabinet to snap to toe height.

  8. Save the Cabinet.

Now when you Place or Auto-Fill with Cabinets they will automatically snap to the toe height. After designing a Job you can then select your Kick Box and it will automatically snap to the floor and fill the space. Just stretch it to the desired length. You can also right click on the Kick Box and copy and paste it. Very quick and easy way to get the job done! If you use this method often, I would also set the Kick Box to appear on my Favorites Bar.

As you know, you can use the Assembly Construction Method to specify when and where you want sleepers in the construction settings. And you can even use UCS’s for the unique cabinet name "Kick Box" to place your laminate or any other modifications that should only happen to the TOE KICK!
