Unfinished Back

This Unfinished Back is the back that is visible within (inside) a Cabinet. The unfinished reference refers to the back of the Back. The options here set whether you have such a Back in your Cabinet, how the back addresses the Wall, I.E, does it touch the Wall or is it held away by a Nailer, or a dimension (Scribe) you define.

The following diagram shows the options available in the Unfinished Back Branch. Click a specific option in the diagram for more information or continue through this topic for information on all the options.

Clipped Back Support Position (Standard #408)

There is a feature under the Unfinished Back branch called the Clipped Back Support. This feature is meant to be used in corner cabinets that have a "clipped back" or an angled back.

Unfinished Back - Clipped Back Support Joint (Standard #409)

The other clipped back support question asks if the joint between the clipped back and the sides is a miter joint or a dado.

Unfinished Back Clipped Back Support Inset (Standard #410)

To apply a Clipped Back Support to a cabinet in a job, you must first edit the shape of the Cabinet so that the back is clipped as desired.

Next, take that Cabinet to the Section Editor (get to the Section Editor by either double clicking the Cabinet from Floor Plan or Elevation view OR by right clicking on the Cabinet and then clicking Edit).

In the Section Editor, you should see the Side Selector in the sidebar.

To apply the clipped back support, click on the back in the side selector and then choose Clipped Back Support for the Back Type in the area below the side selector.
