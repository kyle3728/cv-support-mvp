Creating a Door with Continuous Pull

You will need to add your continuous Pull Material into your Material Catalog. Price the Material "Per Foot" this option prices per lineal foot. Your continuous Pull Material could be some kind of aluminum extrusion, or it could be some kind of wood C-Pull or J-Pull. You would then setup a Door Material Schedule that uses that Material for the Top and/or Bottom Rail.

If you want the continuous Pull to be visually correct on the screen, you will need to create an Outside Edge Profile that looks like your continuous Pull.

  1. Click on the "Create A New Door Style" button.

  2. Name the Door.

  3. Select a Category to store the Door on, use the "Slab" category.

  4. Click on the "Construction" tab.

  5. Click in the middle of the Door and select "Slab" as the Panel Type for this Door.

  6. Add a Top Rail to the Door.

  7. Click on the Top Rail and make the width equal to the width of your continuous Pull.

  8. Click on the Panel and check to make sure the size is correct based on the size of your continuous Pull.

  9. Click on the "Profiles" tab and select the appropriate Profiles to use with this Door.

Across the top of the "Profiles" tab you will note that there is an area called Â "Apply Outside Edge Profile To". This area allows you to select which edges of the Door the Outside Edge Profile will be applied to. If you want the shape of the continuous Pull to only show across the top of the Door, check "Top of Door" and disable the other options.

  1. Click on the "Materials" tab and select the appropriate Material Schedules to use with this Door. Be sure to select Material Schedules that have the correct Material selected for "Slab" and for the Top and/or Bottom Rail.

On your Door cut-list it will list a Top Rail the correct length to cut your continuous Pull, and it will list the Material to make it out of, as your continuous Pull Material. It will also list a slab that is the correct size so that when you add the continuous Pull you will end up with the correct overall Door size.
