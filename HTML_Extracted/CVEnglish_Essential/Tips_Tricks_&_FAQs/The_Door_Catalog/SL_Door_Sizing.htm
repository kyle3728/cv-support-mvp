Door Sizing

Door sizing refers to the overall size of a finished Door, not to the dimensions of the individual parts of the Door.  If you are searching for information on dimensioning of the component Parts, see the Door Catalog.

Doors are sized in two ways, depending on the Construction Style of the Assembly.

For Face Frame Cabinets, the Doors are sized according to the following formula.

Clear opening of the Face Frame +  Door Overlay =  Door size. Drawer Fronts adopt the same Overlay as the Door.

Overlay is a property of a Hinge. As such, it is found on the Hinge Tab of the Material Manager. A single Door that was to cover a 20" x 20" Face Frame opening, using a Hinge with a 1/2" overlay, would produce a Door that was 21" x 21".

The Frameless Style Assemblies do not use overlay to size Doors. These Assemblies look to the values you have entered in the Assembly Wizard for Reveals to determine the Door and Drawer face size. The formula for sizing these Doors is as follows:

Overall Asembly dimension - Reveals = Door Size(s).

A 20" (overall dimension) Assembly with 1/8" reveals all around, would produce a 19 3/4" x 19 3/4" Door.
