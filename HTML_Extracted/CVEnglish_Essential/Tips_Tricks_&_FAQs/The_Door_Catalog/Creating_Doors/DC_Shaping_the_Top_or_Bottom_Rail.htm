Shaping the Top or Bottom Rail

To shape the Top or Bottom Rail.

  1. Make sure the Rail is the correct size.

  2. Right click on the Rail and select "Custom Shape".

If you only want the picture to look good and you don't care if the parts are right you can just select "Arch". Solid will arch the Door for you. However the Parts might not be correct.

Right clicking on the rail and selecting "Custom Shape" will bring you to the Part Shaper. Your screen should look something like this. See the Door Catalog Shape Editor topic for more information on the Shape Editor tools.

This is the Top Rail, so I will be shaping the bottom edge. For this sample I will do a cathedral arch. To do the cathedral arch I will need to add two points one on each side of the Rail and then arc the center line between the two points. In the following picture you can see I added two points using the "Add Points" tool.

Notice that the points I added don't line up with the bottom of the bottom rail. I will use the "Reference Point" tool to line them up.

I  click on the "Reference Point" tool and then I click on the lower left hand dot to make it the reference point. I then right click on the left dot I added and I will get a "Point Position" window.  I then select "Reference Point" relative and type in the positional information for this point.  When I click on "OK" Solid will place the dot where I want it to go.

X is left and right.

Y is up and down.

After both side points are positioned correctly I will use the "Arc Line" tool to arch the line between them up.  I click on the "Arc Line" tool and then click on the line between the two dots and move it up. When I click it will place a third point. This third point controls the arch. The size of my Door panel will be controlled by the distance from the top edge of the Top Rail to the center of the arch on the Top Rail. In my case this would be 2 1/8".

Whenever you have a shaped part and you re-size that shaped part, the part shape will distort. If the top rail distorts it will affect the size of my Door panel and I can't have that. So I am going to use the "Reference Point" tool to align the third dot exactly centered from left to right, and in my case 2 1/8" down from the top of the Top Rail.  I will then use the lock point tool to keep this point from moving when the Door re-sizes.  This will insure that my Door panel will always be the correct size.

In the above picture I clicked on the "Lock Point" tool and then clicked on the center dot.  I will lock it to be centered from left to right and I will lock it to the top so it is always 2 1/8" down from the top. This will insure that my Door panel will always be the correct size. I will not lock the two side points, as I want them to move. When I'm done I click on the "Close" Button.
