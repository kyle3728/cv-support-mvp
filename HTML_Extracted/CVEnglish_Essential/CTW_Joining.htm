Joining

The following diagram shows the options available in the Joining object. Click a specific option in the diagram for more information or continue through this topic for information on all the Joining options.

90 Corner Split (Standard #15)

There are four options for 90 Corner Split:

  • Miter

  • Butt

  • Miter / Butt Combo - Enter the Length of your Miter.

  • Extended Butt - Enter the Length of your Extension.

45 Corner Split (Standard #16)

There are four options for 45 Corner Split:

  • Miter

  • Split Angle

  • Extend One Leg - Enter the Length of your Extension.

  • Extend Both - Enter the Length of your Extension.

Joint Hardware Type (Standard #17)

There are five options for Joint Hardware Type:

  • None

  • Draw Bolt - Select this option to define positioning and machining for Draw Bolt (Tite-Joint) Style fasteners.

  • Dog Bone - Select this option to define positioning and machining for Dog Bone style fasteners.

  • Double Action - Select this option to define positioning and machining for Double Action style fasteners.

  • I-Joint - Select this option to define the Intelli-Joint to use for fastening and to define its positioning.

Hardware Inset From Front Edge (Standard #18)

Enter the distance from the front edge of the Top to the center of the hardware.

Hardware Inset From Back Edge (Standard #19)

Enter the distance from the back edge of the Top to the center of the hardware.

Maximum Hardware Span (Standard #20)

Enter the maximum space you will allow between hardware before adding additional hardware.

Channel Length / Hardware Width (Standard #21)

Enter the full length of the channel for the Draw Bolt.

Channel Width / Bore Diameter / Hardware Depth (Standard #22)

Enter the width of the wide channel for the Draw Bolt fastener.

OR (If Dog Bone)

Enter the Diameter of the boring hole the Dog Bone fastener.

OR (If Double Action or Intelli-Joint)

Enter the depth of a bounding box surrounding the hardware.

Draw Bolt Channel Length (Standard #24)

Enter the length of the narrow channel for one side of the Draw Bolt.

Draw Bolt Channel Width (Standard #25)

Enter the width of the narrow channel for the Draw Bolt.

Draw Bolt Channel Depth (Standard #26)

Enter the depth of the Draw Bolt channel.

Dog Bone Channel Width (Standard #27)

Enter the width of the channel for the Dog Bone.

Dog Bone Channel Depth (Standard #28)

Enter the depth of the channel for the Dog Bone.

Dog Bone Bore Center (Standard #29)

Enter the distance to the bore center.

Dog Bone Bore Depth (Standard #30)

Enter the bore depth for the Dog Bone.

OR

Enter the bore depth for the Double Action.

Double Action Bore Span (Standard #31)

Enter the space between the boring holes for the Double Action hardware.

Double Action Bore Offset (Standard #32)

Enter the offset between the joint and the center of the boring holes.

Double Action Bore Diameter (Standard #33)

Enter the diameter of the boring holes.

Double Action Bore Depth (Standard #34)

Enter the bore depth of the holes.

Hardware Conflict (Standard #35)

In the event your hardware encroaches on your build-up, how do you want to handle the conflict?

You have three options for Hardware Conflict:

  • Ignore Conflict

  • Trim Build-Up

  • Notch Build-Up
