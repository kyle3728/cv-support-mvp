Assembly Sheet -

Assembly sheets are special Reports designed for your assemblers. They will typically have a picture of a single Assembly in the upper right corner of the printout, with all the Parts that go to that Assembly down the left side of the printout, and all the specifications for that Assembly along the bottom of the printout. See the Assembly Sheet Layout section later in this topic for more information.

To Print Assembly Sheet for one Assembly in a Room, select the Assembly to print, and then click on Print.

To Print Assembly Sheets for only some of the Assemblies in a Room; select the Assemblies to print with your mouse while holding down , and then click on Print.

To Print Assembly Sheets for all Assemblies in the Job, select the "Select All" option and then click on Print.

The Assembly Sheets Layout

There are four main sections of the Assembly Sheet Template that ships with Solid.

Cut List Data Table (in Red)

This area of the Assembly Sheet displays the Cut List for your Assembly. This list also will include Hardware included in the Assembly as well as any Comments that have been added to the Assembly.

Container (in Green)

This area will display a graphic of the Primary Face of the Assembly. It also, at the top, includes the Assembly number, Name, Size, and Quantity.

Assembly Properties (in Blue)

This area displays general Assembly Properties.

When values for Scribes are displayed it is important to note that when an Interior Scribes is noted the value for the scribe will be in parenthesis.
