Measure & Snap Marks

The Measure option is available for use in determining the dimension of, or distance between Parts, Objects, etc. When used in conjunction with the Snap Marks option it provides accurate information for Assembly and Part sizes.

The image below shows many of the features of and information provided by the Measure option. Additionally, the Snap to Marks option was selected from statusbar menu (Notice the dark dots in the corners of the Assembly and Assembly Parts in the Plan view, this is indicative of the Snap to Marks option being active).

Here, Measure was used to measure the diagonal of the Assembly by clicking in the rear corner and then clicking in the front corner. A blue line represents the line of the measurement. Notice that the sidebar provides a wealth of information about the measurement; it provides the length of the line (Distance), the Horizontal (Yellow) and Vertical (Green) travel and the angle of the line.

Use of the Snap Marks option instead of the Measure option allows red marks to be placed, and then used as measurement monuments for setting other dimensions.

Tips

You can place Points by Snapping to the Grid Points, Assembly Points or with the Snap Mode turned OFF.

  • Snap marks allows you to place Points

  • Measure allows you to take precise measurements

  • Horizontal: Enter the horizontal position where you want the Point to appear.

  • Vertical: Enter the vertical position where you want the Point to appear.

OR:

  • Enter the Distance and Angle at which you want the Point to appear.

  • Lock Angle is used when you use the keyboard to input the Distance and Angle.

Tips for working with Measure:

  • After manually placing the first point with the mouse, use to move between the Horizontal/Vertical fields in the sidebar.

  • Type in the value and then press to move to the next field.

  • DO NOT press until you are satisfied that your points are correct.

  • Typing in the value and using allows you to visually confirm that the placement of your points is correct before you press .

  • Using the keyboard allows you to achieve an accuracy you can not achieve with the mouse.

  • Use "Snap Marks" to place points.

  • Zoom In or Out while in Ruler Tool mode.

  • After placing a Point or Measuring, right-click to release the cursor.

  • To delete points: Right click on the screen, select Properties then click the Reset Ruler button - this will remove Snap Points.
