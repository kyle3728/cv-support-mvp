Overview of Editing Assemblies

An understanding of the Hierarchy of Casebuilder is necessary before you will fully appreciate the editing potential of Casebuilder. Assembly Editing requires an understanding of the various areas that editing is possible, as well as which areas allow for Assemblies or Parts to retain their logic (I.E., stretch, etc). Editing is the process of sectioning an Assembly or object so that it acquires the look and status of the new Assembly. Typically, this is accomplished by two methods of editing. Section editing is the process where an Assembly is divided into vertical or horizontal areas that are assigned properties, such as Door, Drawer, or Roll Out. Orthographic editing is the process of adding or replacing Parts, and thereafter re-establishing intelligence, or allowing an Override on that Part to exist. These two methods also represent the  two main editing views:

Section Editing - This allows for intelligent editing of the Face and interior (Case) of an Assembly. This also includes menu options for rapidly changing Assembly Properties and attributes. Section Editing is the favored method of Assembly editing. It allows for modifications to the Assembly Face as well as the Assembly interior (Case). All editing performed in the Section Editing views retains intelligence with the Assembly. The retention of intelligence allows the edited Part or area to respond to changes in an Assemblies dimensions. Access to the "Section Face" view is gained via the Section tab, and the Section Face option.

The Section Case option is typical to the above graphic, with the exception that the Section Interior option is selected while in the section window, the Section tab must be clicked before either the "Section Face" or "Section Interior" Section tools appear.

Section Editing is a feature of the Assembly Level of Casebuilder. Therefore the changes you are making to the Assembly in this level are limited to that Assembly, and will not appear on other Assemblies within the Job. If you desire the changes to be available for additional Assemblies, consider saving the Assembly to your library.
